TON 68 is one of the most massive and brightest known quasars in the universe. A quasar is an extremely luminous object powered by a supermassive black hole at the center of a distant galaxy.

TON 68 is located about 10.4 billion light-years away from Earth in the direction of the constellation Canes Venatici. This means the light we see from TON 68 today started traveling when the universe was much younger.

At the center of TON 68 is a supermassive black hole with an estimated mass of about 66 billion times the mass of the Sun. This makes it one of the most massive black holes ever discovered.

The black hole in TON 68 is actively consuming large amounts of gas and matter. As this material falls into the black hole, it heats up and produces enormous amounts of energy, making the quasar extremely bright.

TON 68 is so bright that it outshines its entire host galaxy. Quasars like TON 68 are among the most energetic objects in the universe.

Despite its enormous mass, the black hole itself cannot be seen directly. Scientists detect TON 68 by observing the intense light emitted by the hot material surrounding the black hole.

Studying TON 68 helps scientists understand how supermassive black holes form and grow, and how galaxies evolve over time.

TON 68 is important for understanding the early universe because its light has traveled for billions of years before reaching Earth.

Quasars like TON 68 show how powerful black holes can shape their surrounding environment and influence galaxy formation.
