Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet that supports life. It is located about 149.6 million kilometers from the Sun, a distance known as 1 Astronomical Unit (AU).

Earth has a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers and is the fifth largest planet in the Solar System. It is classified as a terrestrial planet because it has a solid, rocky surface.

About 71% of Earth's surface is covered with water, mostly in oceans, and the remaining 29% is land consisting of continents and islands. This water is essential for supporting life.

Earth has one natural satellite called the Moon. The Moon affects ocean tides and helps stabilize Earth's rotation.

Earth's atmosphere is made mostly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), with small amounts of other gases. This atmosphere protects life by blocking harmful radiation and maintaining temperature.

Earth rotates on its axis once every 24 hours, which causes day and night. It revolves around the Sun once every 365.25 days, which causes seasons.

Earth has a strong magnetic field that protects it from harmful solar radiation and charged particles from the Sun.

Earth is the only known planet where liquid water exists on the surface and where life naturally occurs. Millions of species of plants, animals, and microorganisms live on Earth.

Earth formed about 4.5 billion years ago and continues to evolve through geological and biological processes.
